\section{Bilevel Weight-Adjustment Framework}
Although the single-level model produces feasible and behaviorally meaningful networks, its objective remains a surrogate for the airline's actual target. The project therefore introduces a bilevel scheme in which the upper level selects the lower-level market weights so as to improve true profitability.

At the conceptual level, the bilevel problem can be written as
\begin{align}
\max_{\alpha,\beta} \quad & \Pi\bigl(x^\star(\alpha,\beta)\bigr) \\
\text{s.t.} \quad & x^\star(\alpha,\beta) \in \arg\min_x \Phi^{\mathrm{LL}}(x;\alpha,\beta),
\end{align}
where $\Pi(\cdot)$ denotes the airline profit and $x^\star(\alpha,\beta)$ is an optimal solution of the lower-level design problem. Rather than solving this model through exact bilevel reformulation, the code implements an iterative adjustment of the weight matrices $\alpha_{od}$ and $\beta_{od}$.

The logic is the following. Starting from uniform weights, the algorithm solves the lower-level model and recovers capacities, market shares, and route assignments. Using these outputs, it computes sensitivity-like updates that compare the current lower-level market valuation with the contribution of each market to the upper-level objective. Markets whose lower-level emphasis is too weak relative to profit are assigned larger weights, while markets that are overemphasized are down-weighted. The update is repeated over several inner iterations and the coefficients are projected onto prescribed bounds to keep the procedure numerically stable.

This interpretation is consistent with the expressions appearing in the implementation, where the matrices \texttt{alfa\_od} and \texttt{beta\_od} are updated through gradient-style steps after alternating solves of the lower-level and single-level models. The bilevel layer therefore acts as a calibration mechanism: it does not modify the feasible network set directly, but reshapes the lower-level objective so that the resulting continuous design tracks profit more closely.

The advantage of this strategy is practical tractability. Exact mixed-integer bilevel formulations with fixed costs, passenger-choice structure, and multicommodity flows are extremely difficult to solve. By moving the complexity to an iterative weight-adjustment procedure, the model preserves a strong link between design choices and profitability without sacrificing the continuous structure of the lower-level optimization.
