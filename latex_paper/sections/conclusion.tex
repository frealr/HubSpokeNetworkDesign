\section{Conclusion}
This draft presents a unified view of the methodology implemented in the project for airline hub-and-spoke network design. The key idea is to replace a difficult discrete design problem with a continuous formulation that still captures the interaction among installed capacity, passenger choice, operating cost, and profit.

The resulting framework has three defining elements: a single-level multiobjective design model with entropy-based market-share allocation, a bilevel mechanism that calibrates market weights to improve profitability, and an iterative reweighted $\ell_1$ approximation that embeds fixed costs through sparsity in capacity variables. Together, these ingredients provide a promising basis for solving larger network design instances than would be practical with exact mixed-integer bilevel models.

The next steps for the paper can build naturally on this scaffold: formal notation, a more explicit derivation of the conic reformulations used for the entropy terms, convergence and computational remarks for the reweighting scheme, and numerical experiments comparing the proposed approach against mixed-integer or heuristic baselines.
