\section{Conclusion}
The revised draft now matches the logic of the TFG more closely. The foundation of the methodology is no longer described as an abstract continuous surrogate, but explicitly as the combination of two ingredients developed in Chapters 2 and 3: reweighted-\(\ell_1\) \emph{sparsity} to emulate fixed deployment costs, and entropy regularization to recover logit demand splits through the optimality conditions of a convex problem.

On that basis, the airline hub-and-spoke model from Chapter 5 is the true single-level starting point of the present work. Its defining features are the private-operator objective, the price-weighted passenger and entropy terms, the hub-specific capacity structure, and the profitability constraints. The bilevel layer is then introduced as an extension that learns market-specific weights \(\alpha_{od}\) and \(\beta_{od}\) so that the lower-level design better reflects true profit.

The next useful step for this manuscript is to make the bridge between the TFG notation and the current code fully formal. In particular, the final version should include a compact mathematical statement of the airline lower-level problem, an explicit definition of the upper-level profit function used in the code, and a clearer discussion of which reweighted fixed-cost terms from the original TFG are kept unchanged and which ones are simplified in the present implementation.
