\section{Computational Scheme}
The previous section states the algorithmic structure at the model level. This section summarizes how that structure is instantiated in the current implementation.

The outer loop is a classical majorization-minimization procedure for sparse deployment costs. A fixed activation term $\kappa\,\mathbf{1}_{\{z>0\}}$ is approximated at iteration $k$ through
\begin{equation}
\kappa \, \frac{z^{(k)}}{z^{(k-1)}+\varepsilon},
\end{equation}
which yields the reweighted penalties that are written to GAMS through the arrays \texttt{s\_prev}, \texttt{sh\_prev}, and \texttt{a\_prev}. In the active airline implementation, the nonsmooth approximation is driven primarily by airport and hub capacities.

Inside that outer loop, \texttt{generate\_data.py} runs an inner calibration loop for the market-wise coefficients. The workflow is:
\begin{itemize}
\item solve the penalized bilevel problem in \texttt{cvx-ll.gms};
\item recover capacities, flows, and market shares from the GAMS outputs;
\item update auxiliary bounds for feasible demand capture;
\item solve the pure lower-level problem in \texttt{cvx-sl.gms};
\item compute gradient-like corrections for \texttt{alfa\_od} and \texttt{beta\_od};
\item project the hyperparameters back to their admissible ranges;
\item update the MM reference capacities for the next outer iteration.
\end{itemize}

The most relevant implementation detail is that two GAMS models are solved repeatedly. The first one, \texttt{cvx-ll.gms}, evaluates the penalty surrogate that mixes upper-level profit and lower-level optimality. The second one, \texttt{cvx-sl.gms}, evaluates the lower-level objective itself. Their outputs are then combined in Python to update the market hyperparameters and to refresh the reweighted fixed-cost approximation.

The resulting procedure is not intended as a globally exact solver. It is a structured successive-approximation method whose practical goal is to maintain the continuous scalability of the single-level model while improving the alignment between sparse deployment decisions and upper-level profit.
