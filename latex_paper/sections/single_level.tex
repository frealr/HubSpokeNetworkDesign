\section{Single-Level Multiobjective Formulation}
This section presents the lower-level problem used inside the bilevel scheme. Let
\begin{equation}
x := \big(s,sh,a,f,f^{\mathrm{ext}},f^{od}_{ij}\big)
\end{equation}
collect the airport-capacity, hub-capacity, link-capacity, market-share, and route-flow variables. The lower level is a continuous airline network design model in which fixed deployment costs are approximated by reweighted penalties and the passenger split between the new airline and the outside option is induced through entropy regularization.

To separate the market-wise bilevel hyperparameters from the scalar penalty coefficient used for deployment costs, we denote the former by $\widehat{\alpha}^{od}$ and $\widehat{\beta}^{od}$. The lower-level market weight for market $(o,d)$ is
\begin{equation}
W^{od} := \widehat{\alpha}^{od}\bigl(p^{od}q^{od}\bigr)^{\widehat{\beta}^{od}},
\end{equation}
where $q^{od}$ and $p^{od}$ denote demand and price. In the code, these quantities correspond to \texttt{alfa\_od} and \texttt{beta\_od}.

The lower-level objective is written as
\begin{equation}
\min_x \; \Phi_{\mathrm{LL}}(x;\widehat{\alpha},\widehat{\beta}) :=
OP(a) + DEP_\beta(s,sh,a) + U_{\mathrm{self}}(x) + U_{\mathrm{ext}}(x) + H_{\mathrm{self}}(x) + H_{\mathrm{ext}}(x),
\end{equation}
where the different terms have the following meaning.

\paragraph{Operating costs.}
The direct operating cost is modeled linearly as
\begin{equation}
OP(a) = \sum_{(i,j)\in\mathcal{A}} c_{o_{ij}} a_{ij}.
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Reweighted deployment costs.}
The deployment term penalizes airport activation, hub activation, and, at the formulation level, link deployment. Its generic structure is
\begin{align}
DEP_\beta(s,sh,a) = \beta \Bigg[
& \sum_{(i,j)\in\mathcal{A}} \frac{c_{A_{ij}} a_{ij}}{a_{ij}^{\mathrm{prev}}+\varepsilon_A}
 + \sum_{i\in\mathcal{S}} \frac{c_{S_i}(s_i+sh_i)}{s_i^{\mathrm{prev}}+sh_i^{\mathrm{prev}}+\varepsilon_S} \nonumber \\
& + \sum_{i\in\mathcal{S}} \lambda \frac{c_{H_i} sh_i}{sh_i^{\mathrm{prev}}+\varepsilon_S}
 + \sum_{(i,j)\in\mathcal{A}} \bar c_{A_{ij}} a_{ij}
 + \sum_{i\in\mathcal{S}} \bar c_{S_i}(s_i+sh_i)
\Bigg].
\end{align}
Here $\beta$ is the scalar penalty coefficient associated with the deployment budget and $\lambda$ modulates the cost of activating hubs. In the current implementation, the reweighted fixed-cost approximation is concentrated on airport and hub capacities, which are the variables that dominate the fixed-cost nonsmoothness in the airline case.

\paragraph{Utility terms.}
The utility contribution of passengers traveling on the new airline is written as
\begin{equation}
U_{\mathrm{self}}(x) =
- \sum_{(o,d)\in\mathcal{K}} W^{od} p^{od}
\left[
\omega_p p^{od} f^{od}
 \omega_t \sum_{(i,j)\in\mathcal{A}} \bigl(t_{ij}+t^{od}_{ij,\mathrm{trans}}\bigr) f_{ij}^{od}
\right],
\end{equation}
whereas the competing outside option contributes
\begin{equation}
U_{\mathrm{ext}}(x) =
- \sum_{(o,d)\in\mathcal{K}} W^{od} p^{od}
\bigl(\omega_t t^{od}_{\mathrm{ext}} + \omega_p p^{od}_{\mathrm{ext}}\bigr)f^{od}_{\mathrm{ext}}.
\end{equation}
The multiplication by $p^{od}$ is essential in the airline setting because the lower level must distinguish not only between large-demand markets, but also between markets that generate different revenue levels.

\paragraph{Entropy terms and logit shares.}
The logit split is induced by the entropy terms
\begin{align}
H_{\mathrm{self}}(x) &= \sum_{(o,d)\in\mathcal{K}} W^{od} p^{od} f^{od}\bigl(\log(f^{od})-1\bigr), \\
H_{\mathrm{ext}}(x) &= \sum_{(o,d)\in\mathcal{K}} W^{od} p^{od} f^{od}_{\mathrm{ext}}
\left(\log\left(\frac{f^{od}_{\mathrm{ext}}}{n_{\mathrm{airlines}}}\right)-1\right).
\end{align}
These terms replace the explicit nonlinear logit constraints that would appear in a classical mixed-integer model and produce the market split through the optimality conditions of the continuous problem.

\paragraph{Constraints.}
The lower-level objective is minimized subject to:
\begin{itemize}
\item link-capacity constraints relating traffic and frequencies;
\item airport-capacity constraints for arrivals and departures;
\item hub-capacity constraints for transfer traffic;
\item direct-flight and connectivity constraints induced by the hub-and-spoke structure;
\item symmetry constraints on operated links;
\item flow-conservation constraints for every market;
\item market split constraints $f^{od}+f^{od}_{\mathrm{ext}}=1$;
\item profitability constraints ensuring that route revenue dominates operating cost;
\item nonnegativity and candidate-link restrictions.
\end{itemize}

This lower-level problem is the model that the bilevel scheme repeatedly solves and reweights. The role of the upper level is not to change the feasible set, but to learn the coefficients $\widehat{\alpha}^{od}$ and $\widehat{\beta}^{od}$ that determine how the lower-level passenger terms value each market.
